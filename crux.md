App Requirements:
QR Code Scanning:
Ability to scan a QR code and retrieve plant information.
QR codes should link to unique plant data within the app.
When scanning a QR code (e.g., for "Zinnia"), the app runs the plant info for that specific plant.
Plant Information:
Each plant should have accurate information, including:
Common name, scientific name, care instructions, and growing conditions.
Planting, watering, and basic information.
Photos of the plant for identification.
Plant Information Examples:
Zinnia:
Name: Zinnia Elegans  
Species: Zinnia  
Native Range: Native to Mexico and Central America  
 Recommended Location: In front of a window  
 Plant Information: Zinnias are fairly quick to flower and provide long-lasting color throughout the summer. They thrive in hot conditions and attract butterflies, and most varieties make excellent cut flowers. Zinnias do best in full sun and well-drained soil. Known as common zinnia, this erect flowering annual belongs to the Asteraceae (aster) family and has Mexican origins. 
Marigold:
Name: Aztec Marigold  
Species: Marigold  
Native Range: Native to Central and Southern Mexico, as well as several other Latin American countries  
Recommended Location: Window  
Plant Information: The Aztec Marigold features dense heads of orange or yellow flowers and is commonly planted in garden borders or as companion plants to deter pests in vegetable gardens. These flowering plants have bright blooms and are part of the sunflower family. Marigolds symbolize life and death in Day of the Dead traditions. In the short story "Marigolds," they represent the aspects of life that make it worth living.
